# Purpose

This document defines the business requirements for the MatchStick Events website.

It explains what business problems the website should solve, what success looks like, who the stakeholders are, and the boundaries of the project.

This document focuses on **business needs**, not technical implementation.

---

# Project Background

MatchStick Events is an event planning company founded in 2012 by **Preksha Chhajer**. The company specializes in creating elegant and memorable celebrations ranging from weddings and milestone events to corporate gatherings.

The client intends to establish a premium online presence that reflects the quality of their work while creating a more professional and trustworthy first impression for prospective clients. 0

---

# Business Problem Statement

At present, most enquiries are received through:

- Word of Mouth
- Instagram Direct Messages
- Phone Calls
- WhatsApp
- Email

While these channels are effective, they do not provide a unified digital presence that showcases the company's portfolio, services, and brand identity. 1

The website should solve this problem by becoming the company's primary digital showcase while continuing to support these existing communication channels.

---

# Primary Business Objectives

The website should achieve the following objectives:

- Build trust with new visitors.
- Present MatchStick Events as a premium event planning brand.
- Showcase completed projects professionally.
- Increase consultation enquiries.
- Improve lead quality.
- Strengthen online brand identity.
- Support long-term business growth.
- Reduce dependence on social media as the sole discovery platform.

---

# Success Metrics (Business KPIs)

The following indicators may be used to measure the website's success:

- Increase in consultation requests.
- Growth in qualified leads.
- Increased portfolio page engagement.
- Longer average session duration.
- Higher conversion from visitor to enquiry.
- Positive client feedback regarding the website experience.

These are recommended business metrics and are **design decisions**, not explicit client requirements.

---

# Stakeholders

## Primary Stakeholder

**Business Owner**

- Preksha Chhajer
- Responsible for approving branding, content and business decisions.

---

## Secondary Stakeholders

- Website visitors
- Potential clients
- Future employees
- Development team
- Designers
- Marketing team

---

# Target Customers

The website should primarily attract:

- Wedding clients
- Families planning anniversaries
- Parents organising birthdays
- Parents planning baby showers
- Corporate organisations
- Luxury event clients
- Individuals seeking bespoke celebrations

These audiences align with the services described in the client brief. 2

---

# Business Scope

The website will include:

- Homepage
- Services
- Gallery
- Previous Events
- Contact
- Consultation / Booking

These pages are explicitly requested by the client. 3

---

# Business Features

The website should enable visitors to:

- Learn about the company.
- Explore services.
- View previous work.
- Discover the brand personality.
- Contact the business.
- Request a consultation.

---

# Business Value of Each Section

## Homepage

Purpose:

Create an exceptional first impression.

---

## Services

Purpose:

Explain what MatchStick Events offers while encouraging visitors to enquire rather than compare prices.

---

## Gallery

Purpose:

Allow visitors to experience the visual quality of the company's work.

---

## Previous Events

Purpose:

Demonstrate real-world experience and establish credibility through completed projects.

The client has provided portfolio examples including events at Taj Bengal, Suryagarh, The Dockyard Co., and JW Marriott. 4

---

## Contact

Purpose:

Reduce friction in getting in touch with the company.

---

## Consultation

Purpose:

Generate qualified leads instead of immediate bookings.

---

# Functional Business Requirements

The website shall:

- Present company information.
- Present services.
- Present previous work.
- Display contact information.
- Enable consultation enquiries.
- Work on mobile devices.
- Represent the luxury identity of the company consistently.

---

# Non-Functional Business Requirements

The website should be:

- Elegant
- Fast
- Reliable
- Professional
- Easy to navigate
- Mobile responsive
- Accessible
- Consistent

The branding direction should remain modern and elegant without becoming visually cluttered. 5

---

# Out of Scope

The following are outside the scope of this project:

- Public pricing.
- E-commerce.
- Online payments.
- Customer account system.
- Instant booking confirmation.
- Marketplace functionality.

These exclusions are based on the client's consultation-driven workflow. 6

---

# Assumptions

The following assumptions are made during documentation:

- High-quality photographs will be available.
- Portfolio content will continue to grow.
- The owner will review website content before launch.
- The company intends to maintain the website after deployment.

These assumptions are **design decisions** and should be validated during implementation.

---

# Risks

Potential business risks include:

- Insufficient portfolio content.
- Outdated event information.
- Slow response to enquiries.
- Inconsistent branding across social platforms.
- Poor mobile experience affecting conversions.

---

# Constraints

Current known constraints include:

- Branding should remain elegant and modern.
- Existing enquiry channels should remain available.
- The website should support, not replace, the current business workflow.
